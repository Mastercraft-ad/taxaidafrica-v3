Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Every Nigerian resident who earns income above the minimum threshold is required to pay taxes. This includes employees, self-employed individuals, and business owners. Non-residents who earn income from Nigerian sources are also liable to pay taxes.

Personal income tax returns must be filed by March 31st of the year following the tax year. For example, taxes for the 2024 tax year must be filed by March 31st, 2025. Late filing may result in penalties.

You can apply for a TIN through the Joint Tax Board's online portal or by visiting your nearest tax office. You'll need to provide valid identification documents and proof of address. Our volunteers can help you through this process.

You'll typically need your TIN, proof of income (pay slips, bank statements), receipts for allowable deductions, and identification documents. For businesses, you'll also need financial statements and VAT records if applicable.
